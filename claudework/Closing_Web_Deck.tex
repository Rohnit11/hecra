% ---------- YANG: SCARCE REGIME ----------
\begin{frame}{When data is scarce, generous fair use \alert{harms} AI development}
\vspace{0.2cm}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Generous fair use} ($f \to 0$): kills creator incentives $\rightarrow$ \alert{no human content} for training $\rightarrow$ model improvement stalls. \emph{(Prop.~10)}
\item \textbf{Strict fair use} ($f$ too large): the AI company \alert{abandons model improvement} entirely. \emph{(Prop.~11)}
\item \textbf{A moderate $f$} maximizes model quality $X_2$, social welfare, and consumer surplus.
\end{itemize}
\vspace{0.4cm}
\begin{block}{The dynamic problem}
A copyright doctrine welfare-optimal in 2020 (data-abundant) becomes welfare-destroying once pre-existing data is exhausted (data-scarce, $Q_0\!=\!0$) \textemdash{} the regime Longpre et al.\ document we are entering.
\end{block}
\vspace{0.1cm}
\begin{flushright}
\footnotesize\textcolor{WarmGray}{Yang \& Zhang (2024), Propositions 10\textendash 11; Figure~7.}
\end{flushright}
\end{frame}

% ---------- YANG: JOINT DESIGN ----------
\begin{frame}{Strong AI-copyrightability flips fair use from helpful to harmful}
\vspace{0.2cm}
\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.25}
\begin{tabular}{@{}>{\columncolor{LightGray}}p{4.0cm}>{\RaggedRight\arraybackslash}p{8.4cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{AI-copyrightability $\phi$} & \textbf{Effect of generous fair use on AI development} \\
\midrule
\textbf{Weak} & \textcolor{Teal}{\textbf{Promotes}} development \\
& \footnotesize creators still produce human content for training \\
\midrule
\textbf{Strong} & \alert{\textbf{Hinders}} development \\
& \footnotesize too few human creators $\rightarrow$ training data dries up \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
\vspace{0.3cm}
$X_2$ is \alert{inverted-U} in $\phi$ when data is scarce \emph{(Prop.~12)}. The two policy levers cannot be designed independently.\\[0.2cm]
\begin{flushright}
\footnotesize\textcolor{WarmGray}{Yang \& Zhang (2024), Figure~9 \& Corollary~2.}
\end{flushright}
\end{frame}

% ---------- DEVIL'S ADVOCATE ----------
\begin{frame}{A skeptic would say the closing web is rhetorical, not real}
\vspace{0.1cm}
\textbf{The objection.} If half the bots ignore strict directives (Kim), if robots.txt is not legally binding, if frontier labs cut bilateral licenses, then ``the web is closing'' is a story about polite, compliant labs \textemdash{} not about training in practice.\\[0.4cm]
\textbf{The response.}
\begin{itemize}
\item \alert{Compliant labs are the regulated mainstream}: ClaudeBot, GPTBot and Amazonbot all hit \textbf{1.000} on disallow-all (Kim Table~6). Restrictions therefore bite hardest on the firms regulators can actually reach.
\item \alert{Spoofing is small but selective}: $<$0.1\% of traffic is spoofed, yet spoofed bots show ``little respect for robots.txt'' (Kim Table~9, Fig.~11) \textemdash{} a perverse-incentive layer above the consent system.
\item \alert{The four pillars are joint constraints}: even if signaling and compliance fail, the supply (Fletcher) and legal (Cooper, Yang) pillars still bind. Ignoring robots.txt does not make a \$1.5B recovery go away.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
