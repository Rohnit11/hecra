# Liu, Zhang & Liang (2023) — Evaluating Verifiability in Generative Search Engines

**Citation.** Liu, N. F., Zhang, T., & Liang, P. (2023). "Evaluating Verifiability in Generative Search Engines." *Findings of the Association for Computational Linguistics: EMNLP 2023*, pp. 7001–7025. Stanford University. Supported in part by the AI2050 program at Schmidt Futures (Grant G-22-63429).

---

## 1. Identification Strategy: Descriptive Human-Evaluation Audit — Not Causal; Cross-System Comparison of 4 Commercial Generative Search Engines on a Fixed 1,450-Query Benchmark

- **Study design: algorithmic audit / descriptive benchmark evaluation** (p.7001, explicitly framed "akin to prior work in algorithmic auditing" citing Metaxas & Pruksachatkun 2017, Buolamwini & Gebru 2018, etc.). **No causal identification strategy** — no RCT, no instrument, no natural experiment. Each of four commercial systems is scraped with the *same* 1,450-query bundle and its responses evaluated by crowd-sourced humans.
- **Four evaluation metrics defined in §2** (p.7002):
  1. **Fluency** (five-point Likert, §2.2) — "The response is fluent and cohesive."
  2. **Perceived Utility** (five-point Likert, §2.2) — "The response is a helpful and informative answer to the query."
  3. **Citation Recall** (§2.3): proportion of verification-worthy statements in response *r* fully supported by their associated citations. Operationalized via Rashkin et al. (2022) **AIS (attributable to identified sources) framework**: statement $s_i$ is fully supported by its citations $C_i$ if a generic hearer would affirm "According to cited webpages $C_i$, $s_i$."
  4. **Citation Precision** (§2.4): $(T_{fs}+T_{ps})/N$, where $T_{fs}$ = citations that fully support their statement, $T_{ps}$ = partial-support citations whose associated statement is fully supported only by the union of its citations and no single citation fully supports it; $N$ = total citations.
  5. **Citation F1** (§2.5): harmonic mean of precision and recall.
- **Unit of analysis**: the (query, system-response) pair. Responses segmented into sentence-level statements; each statement's associated citations ("[1]", "[2]", …) graded **full / partial / no / inaccessible / contradictory / unclear** support (six-option dropdown — p.7018).
- **Three-step crowd protocol** (§3.3, p.7006; Figs. 5–7 pp.7013–14): (i) fluency + perceived-utility rating; (ii) filter non-verification-worthy statements (speaker self-reference, questions to user, hypotheticals); (iii) per-citation support judgment.
- **The "shock"** (audit-inferred): the paper does not exploit any shock; it is a point-in-time snapshot of four systems (responses scraped late Feb–late Mar 2023). Cross-system and cross-query-distribution comparisons are *associations*, not treatment effects.

## 2. Data & Sample: 4 Commercial Generative Search Engines × 1,450 Queries Spanning 12 Distributions; 34 MTurk Annotators; Inter-Annotator F1 ≥ 91

- **Systems audited** (§3.1, p.7004): **Bing Chat**, **NeevaAI**, **perplexity.ai**, **YouChat**. ChatGPT and Bard excluded because they lacked in-line citations as of March 2023.
- **Abstention rates** (Table 1, p.7005): Bing Chat <0.5%, **NeevaAI 22.7%**, perplexity.ai <0.5%, YouChat <0.5%. NeevaAI abstains because responses are displayed inside a conventional SERP.
- **Query bundle**: **1,450 queries per system** drawn from 12 distributions (§3.2, pp.7005–7006):
  - 150 queries each from: **AllSouls** (Oxford entrance-exam general-paper questions), **davinci-debate** (150 debate questions generated by prompting `text-davinci-003` from Perspectrum/Chen 2019 seeds; §B.1, p.7012), **ELI5 (KILT)** (Reddit ELI5 pre-July-2018 from Fan 2019 / Petroni 2021), **ELI5 (Live)** (new ELI5 posts streamed and queried immediately to avoid index contamination), **WikiHowKeywords** (WikiHow article titles paraphrased into search queries by `text-davinci-003`; §B.3 p.7012).
  - 100 queries each for **7 NaturalQuestions subdistributions** (Kwiatkowski et al. 2019), stratified by long-answer format (paragraph/list/table × with/without short answer) plus a "no long answer" bucket.
- **Scraping window**: late February 2023 – late March 2023 (§3.1, p.7004). Only the **first complete single-turn response** is evaluated.
- **Annotators**: **34 total participated** (acknowledgements, p.7010). Amazon Mechanical Turk. Qualification study + personalized feedback; constant contact. Compensation $1.00 per response-with-citations / $0.38 per response-without ($15/hr target). Mean 4 minutes per task (§3.3, p.7006).
- **Inter-annotator agreement** (Table 4, p.7020; 250 triple-annotated pairs):
  - Fluency — 88.5% pairwise, F1 = 94.1
  - Perceived Utility — 86.4%, F1 = 93.1
  - Verifiability (statement-requires-citation) — 94.6%, F1 = 97.3
  - Citation Supports — 82.0%, F1 = 91.0
  - Statement Supported (union of cites) — 82.2%, F1 = 91.1
- **Not a sample, a full-coverage benchmark** per system. The "N" for population-level claims is thus 4 × 1,450 = **5,800 query-response pairs**, minus NeevaAI's 22.7% abstentions.

## 3. Main Findings: 51.5% Recall & 74.5% Precision on Average; Citation Precision Inversely Correlated With Perceived Utility at r = −0.96

All numbers below are from the paper's Tables 5–8 (pp.7021–7024) and Abstract/§4 (pp.7001, 7006–7008). The paper reports **no confidence intervals, standard errors, or p-values** — only averages of ordinal Likert ratings and % rates.

### A. Headline verifiability results (Abstract + §4.2, p.7007)

- **Average citation recall across all systems = 51.5%** (only just over half of generated statements are fully supported by their citations).
- **Average citation precision across all systems = 74.5%** (roughly a quarter of citations fail to support their associated statement).

### B. Per-system averages (Tables 7 & 8, pp.7023–7024)

| System | Recall (%) | Precision (%) | Fluency (1–5) | Perceived Utility (1–5) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bing Chat | 58.7 | **89.5** (highest) | 4.40 (lowest) | 4.34 (lowest) |
| NeevaAI | 67.6 | 72.0 | 4.43 | 4.48 |
| perplexity.ai | **68.7** (highest) | 72.7 | 4.51 | 4.56 |
| YouChat | 11.1 (lowest) | 63.6 (lowest) | 4.59 (highest) | **4.62** (highest) |
| **Average** | **51.5** | **74.5** | **4.48** | **4.50** |

- **Recall spread: ~58 pp** between perplexity.ai (68.7) and YouChat (11.1).
- **Precision spread: ~26 pp** between Bing Chat (89.5) and YouChat (63.6).

### C. Inverse correlation between precision and perceived utility (§4.3, p.7008)

- **Pearson r = −0.96** between average citation precision and average perceived utility across the four systems (Figure 3, p.7008).
- Concrete example — the Bing Chat response to "Is cooperation or competition the driving force guiding the evolution of society?" copies *biological*-evolution text verbatim (high precision) but answers the *societal*-evolution query poorly (low utility) — Figure 4, p.7008.

### D. Generative systems closely paraphrase/copy cited webpages (Table 2, p.7009)

Similarity between each generated statement and the annotator-extracted evidence span:

| System | BLEU | BERTScore (F1) |
|---|---|---|
| Bing Chat | 44.1 | 78.8 |
| NeevaAI | 30.0 | 72.9 |
| perplexity.ai | 22.3 | 69.2 |
| YouChat | 28.6 | 72.0 |
| **Average** | **31.3** | **73.2** |

- **r = 0.80** between similarity metrics (BLEU / BERTScore) and average citation precision — systems that copy more text from cited pages get higher precision.

### E. Query-distribution heterogeneity (Tables 5–8 appendices; text pp.7007)

- **Citation recall is much more sensitive to query distribution than citation precision is.**
  - NQ queries *with* a long answer: avg recall 58.5% vs. non-NQ distributions: 47.8% (~11 pp gap).
  - Recall on open-ended AllSouls essay questions is lowest (44.3%) — retrieval fails to surface direct extractive evidence.
  - Highest-recall cell: perplexity.ai on NQ list-long / has-short-answer = **85.3%** (Table 7).
  - Lowest-recall cell: YouChat on ELI5 (KILT) = **3.0%** (Table 7).
- **Precision is highest on NQ paragraph-long / has-short-answer queries (81.5%)**, lowest on AllSouls (67.8%) and davinci-debate (70.3%) (Table 8). Highest cell: Bing Chat on NQ paragraph-long / has-short = 92.9%; lowest cell: YouChat on davinci-debate = 50.0%.
- **Fluency is uniformly high** (system averages 4.40–4.59; all-query avg 4.48) with the only notable dip on **NQ table-long / no-short-answer (avg 4.36)** where synthesis across table cells is required.

### F. Perceived utility heterogeneity (Table 6, p.7022)

- Perceived utility is higher on NQ-with-long-answer (4.59) than on non-NQ distributions (4.43), and decreases as queries demand longer-form, less-extractive answers (short-answer NQ = 4.62; ELI5-style long-form consistently lower).

## 4. Limitations (Explicit + Audit-Inferred)

Author-acknowledged limitations (Limitations section, p.7010):

- **Verifiability ≠ factuality.** A statement with a fully-supporting citation can still be factually wrong (if the cited source is wrong); a verifiable system is not automatically a truthful system.
- **Sentence-level segmentation is coarse.** Sentences often contain multiple claims ("Cats[1] and dogs[2] are common pets."); no consensus linguistic definition of a "claim," so authors default to sentence-granularity for reproducibility. Finer-grained claim-level evaluation is deferred.
- **Single-citation scope ambiguity** (§2.1, p.7003) — a cite marker after two statements can support either only the latter or both.
- **Users may not bother to check sources** regardless of recall/precision, given the "facade of trustworthiness" generated by high fluency/utility.

Audit-inferred limitations for downstream use:

- **Snapshot in Feb–Mar 2023** — predates GPT-4-era Bing Chat upgrades, Google AI Overviews (2024), ChatGPT search (late 2024), and the Gemini / Claude answer surfaces. All four audited systems' behavior has likely drifted (e.g., NeevaAI was shut down in May 2023). **Do not cite these specific 51.5% / 74.5% numbers as current state** without re-auditing.
- **No causal identification** — cannot be cited for effects of any intervention (prompt design, retrieval change, model scale) on verifiability. Cross-system differences are descriptive.
- **Query distributions are US-/English-centric**: Google NQ, Oxford AllSouls, Reddit ELI5, WikiHow, debate prompts from `text-davinci-003`. No multilingual or non-Western evaluation.
- **Single-turn only** (p.7004) — multi-turn refinements where users could ask follow-ups are unevaluated.
- **Each query-response pair annotated once** (except the 250-pair agreement subsample) — no individual-level uncertainty quantification; inter-annotator F1 ≥ 91 but no SE on recall/precision means.
- **"Verification-worthy" scope is maximalist** — authors treat all external-world statements as citation-requiring, including "common sense" claims (p.7016); an alternative protocol that excluded trivially-known claims would likely raise recall numbers.

## 5. Key Implications for the Broader Research Agenda

- **Benchmark / metric contribution**: operationalizes the **citation-recall / citation-precision / citation-F1** triple as a reproducible evaluation framework for any system that generates text with in-line cites. Released annotations at `github.com/nelson-liu/evaluating-verifiability-in-generative-search-engines`.
- **Foundational finding #1 — low verifiability under a facade of fluency.** 51.5% recall and 74.5% precision at fluency 4.48/5 establishes the empirical backbone of later "LLM hallucination in search" arguments (anticipated by Potthast–Hagen–Stein 2020's "direct answer dilemma" opinion piece).
- **Foundational finding #2 — precision–utility trade-off (r = −0.96).** Systems that copy or paraphrase cited text are more verifiable but less useful; systems that paraphrase/synthesize feel more helpful but cite less faithfully. This is a direct empirical instantiation of the Ladhak et al. 2022 faithfulness-vs-abstractiveness trade-off, now measured in a user-facing product setting.
- **Design implication**: increasing retrieval quality (more relevant webpages) should raise recall (since systems struggle to cite when retrieval fails — AllSouls open-ended example), whereas training systems to *cite* more comprehensively should raise precision; the two levers are distinct.
- **Benchmark reuse**: the 1,450-query bundle and distribution design (AllSouls, davinci-debate, ELI5-Live, WikiHowKeywords, 7 NQ stratifications) has become a template for post-2023 generative-search evaluations.
- **Policy / user-facing**: the inverse precision–utility correlation implies that user-perceived quality is *not* a reliable signal of trustworthiness — the opposite, in fact — which has direct bearing on UI-disclosure and regulatory discussions around AI-generated answers.
